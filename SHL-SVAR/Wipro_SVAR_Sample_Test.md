## Section 4: Grammar / Sentence Correction (Sample)

### Identify the error and speak the corrected sentence:

```
1. Wrong: "He go to office every day."
   Correct: "He GOES to office every day."
   (Subject-verb agreement)

2. Wrong: "She is more smarter than her brother."
   Correct: "She is SMARTER than her brother."
   (Double comparative)

3. Wrong: "I have went to the market yesterday."
   Correct: "I WENT to the market yesterday."
   (Tense error - simple past, not present perfect)

4. Wrong: "Each students must complete their project."
   Correct: "Each STUDENT must complete HIS/HER project."
   (Singular subject agreement)

5. Wrong: "He don't know nothing about the project."
   Correct: "He DOESN'T KNOW ANYTHING about the project."
   (Double negative + subject-verb agreement)

6. Wrong: "The informations provided were incorrect."
   Correct: "The INFORMATION provided was incorrect."
   (Uncountable noun - no plural)

7. Wrong: "I am working here since 2019."
   Correct: "I HAVE BEEN WORKING here since 2019."
   (Since requires present perfect continuous)

8. Wrong: "She suggested me to apply for the job."
   Correct: "She suggested THAT I apply for the job."
   (Suggest + that clause)

9. Wrong: "If I will study hard, I will pass the exam."
   Correct: "If I STUDY hard, I will pass the exam."
   (Conditional Type 1 - no will in if clause)

10. Wrong: "He is one of the best player in the team."
    Correct: "He is one of the best PLAYERS in the team."
    (One of the + plural noun)

11. Wrong: "I and my friend went to the movie."
    Correct: "MY FRIEND AND I went to the movie."
    (Pronoun order - I comes last)

12. Wrong: "The meeting is in 3 o'clock."
    Correct: "The meeting is AT 3 o'clock."
    (Preposition of time)

13. Wrong: "He has been absent since three days."
    Correct: "He has been absent FOR three days."
    (Since = point in time, For = duration)

14. Wrong: "She told that she was busy."
    Correct: "She SAID that she was busy."
    (Told requires an object - told me/him)

15. Wrong: "I am agree with your opinion."
    Correct: "I AGREE with your opinion."
    (Agree is a verb, not adjective - no 'am')
```

### Set B — Tense & Verb Errors
```
16. Wrong: "He has completed the work yesterday."
    Correct: "He COMPLETED the work yesterday."
    (Yesterday = past time marker, use simple past, not present perfect)

17. Wrong: "She is knowing the answer."
    Correct: "She KNOWS the answer."
    (Know is a stative verb - no continuous form)

18. Wrong: "When I will reach there, I will call you."
    Correct: "When I REACH there, I will call you."
    (Time clause with 'when' — no future tense)

19. Wrong: "He did not came to the office today."
    Correct: "He did not COME to the office today."
    (After did/did not, use base form of verb)

20. Wrong: "The work has been did by the team."
    Correct: "The work has been DONE by the team."
    (Past participle of 'do' is 'done', not 'did')
```

### Set C — Subject-Verb Agreement
```
21. Wrong: "The team are working on the project."
    Correct: "The team IS working on the project."
    (Collective noun — singular verb in Indian/British context)

22. Wrong: "Neither the manager nor the employees was present."
    Correct: "Neither the manager nor the employees WERE present."
    (Neither...nor — verb agrees with nearest subject)

23. Wrong: "Mathematics are my favorite subject."
    Correct: "Mathematics IS my favorite subject."
    (Subject names ending in -s but singular in meaning)

24. Wrong: "Every employee have to submit their ID proof."
    Correct: "Every employee HAS to submit THEIR ID proof."
    (Every + singular noun = singular verb)

25. Wrong: "One of my friends are working at Wipro."
    Correct: "One of my friends IS working at Wipro."
    (One of + plural noun = singular verb)
```

### Set D — Articles & Determiners
```
26. Wrong: "He is a honest man."
    Correct: "He is AN honest man."
    (Silent 'h' — use 'an' before vowel sound)

27. Wrong: "She went to the abroad for higher studies."
    Correct: "She went ABROAD for higher studies."
    (No article before 'abroad')

28. Wrong: "I need a information about the course."
    Correct: "I need INFORMATION about the course."
    (Uncountable noun — no article 'a/an')

29. Wrong: "He is best player in the team."
    Correct: "He is THE best player in the team."
    (Superlative adjective requires 'the')

30. Wrong: "She plays a piano very well."
    Correct: "She plays THE piano very well."
    (Musical instruments take 'the')
```

### Set E — Preposition Errors
```
31. Wrong: "He is married with a doctor."
    Correct: "He is married TO a doctor."
    (Married to, not married with)

32. Wrong: "She is afraid from dogs."
    Correct: "She is afraid OF dogs."
    (Afraid of, not afraid from)

33. Wrong: "I congratulated him for his success."
    Correct: "I congratulated him ON his success."
    (Congratulate on, not congratulate for)

34. Wrong: "He prevented me to go there."
    Correct: "He prevented me FROM going there."
    (Prevent from + gerund)

35. Wrong: "She is superior than her colleagues."
    Correct: "She is superior TO her colleagues."
    (Superior/inferior/senior/junior take 'to', not 'than')
```

### Set F — Word Order & Structure
```
36. Wrong: "He speaks very well English."
    Correct: "He speaks English very well."
    (Object before adverb in English)

37. Wrong: "She told me that where is the office."
    Correct: "She told me where the office IS."
    (Indirect question — no inversion)

38. Wrong: "I have a five years old daughter."
    Correct: "I have a five-YEAR-old daughter."
    (Compound adjective — no plural)

39. Wrong: "He is enough smart to solve this problem."
    Correct: "He is smart ENOUGH to solve this problem."
    (Enough comes after adjective)

40. Wrong: "She asked me that what is my name."
    Correct: "She asked me what my name WAS."
    (Indirect speech — no 'that' with wh-questions, past tense)
```

### Set G — Common Indian English Mistakes
```
41. Wrong: "He said me to come early."
    Correct: "He TOLD me to come early."
    (Say cannot take indirect object directly)

42. Wrong: "I am having two brothers."
    Correct: "I HAVE two brothers."
    (Have for possession — not continuous)

43. Wrong: "She asked that can she leave early."
    Correct: "She asked WHETHER she COULD leave early."
    (Indirect question — no inversion, use 'whether')

44. Wrong: "He gave me an advice."
    Correct: "He gave me ADVICE."
    (Advice is uncountable — no 'an'; use 'a piece of advice')

45. Wrong: "I am working in Wipro since three years."
    Correct: "I HAVE BEEN WORKING AT Wipro FOR three years."
    (Since → for; in → at; am working → have been working)

46. Wrong: "He is more taller than his brother."
    Correct: "He is TALLER than his brother."
    (Comparative — use -er OR more, never both)

47. Wrong: "Where I can find the library?"
    Correct: "Where CAN I find the library?"
    (Question word + auxiliary + subject)

48. Wrong: "He do not has any experience."
    Correct: "He DOES NOT HAVE any experience."
    (Does + not + base form)

49. Wrong: "The furnitures in the office are new."
    Correct: "The FURNITURE in the office IS new."
    (Furniture is uncountable — no plural)

50. Wrong: "She is working here from January."
    Correct: "She HAS BEEN WORKING here SINCE January."
    (From → since; is working → has been working)
```

> **Quick Grammar Rules to Remember:**
> - **Since** = point in time (since Monday, since 2020)
> - **For** = duration (for three days, for two years)
> - **Say** = no indirect object ("He said that...") | **Tell** = indirect object ("He told me that...")
> - **Stative verbs** (know, have, believe, understand) = NO continuous form
> - **Superior/inferior/senior/junior** = use "to" NOT "than"
> - **Uncountable nouns** (information, advice, furniture, luggage) = NO plural, NO "a/an"

## Section 6: Topic Speaking (30 sec Think + 45 sec Speak)

### Sample Topic 1: "Why is communication important in the workplace?"
```
30-sec Plan: (1) Team collaboration (2) Avoid misunderstandings (3) Career growth

"I believe communication is extremely important in the workplace.
Firstly, effective communication helps team members collaborate
efficiently. When everyone clearly understands their roles and
responsibilities, projects run smoothly and deadlines are met.
Secondly, good communication helps avoid misunderstandings and
conflicts. For example, a simple miscommunication in an email
can lead to costly errors in a project. Moreover, employees who
communicate well are more likely to be noticed by management and
get promoted to leadership positions. In conclusion, strong
communication skills are essential for both individual career
growth and overall organizational success."
```

### Sample Topic 2: "Describe your favorite hobby"
```
30-sec Plan: (1) What hobby (2) Why I enjoy it (3) What I learned

"My favorite hobby is reading books, and I try to dedicate at
least thirty minutes to it every day. I particularly enjoy reading
technology-related books and self-improvement books because they
help me stay updated and grow as a person. For instance, I recently
read a book about effective habits which taught me the importance
of time management and consistency. Reading also improves my
vocabulary and communication skills, which is very helpful in
my professional life. Additionally, it helps me relax after a
long day of work. To sum up, reading is not just a hobby for me
but a habit that contributes to my personal and professional
development."
```

### Sample Topic 3: "Impact of social media on youth"
```
30-sec Plan: (1) Positive - connectivity (2) Negative - addiction (3) Balance needed

"Social media has a significant impact on today's youth, both
positive and negative. On the positive side, platforms like
LinkedIn and Twitter help young people connect with professionals,
learn new skills, and stay informed about current events. Many
students use YouTube and online communities for educational
purposes. However, on the negative side, excessive use of social
media can lead to addiction, reduced productivity, and mental
health issues such as anxiety and low self-esteem. For example,
many young people spend hours scrolling through Instagram instead
of focusing on their studies or career. Therefore, I believe it
is important for youth to use social media in a balanced and
productive manner. In conclusion, social media is a powerful tool
that should be used wisely."
```

### Sample Topic 4: "Advantages of working in a team"
```
30-sec Plan: (1) Diverse skills (2) Shared workload (3) Learning opportunity

"Working in a team offers numerous advantages in both professional
and academic settings. Firstly, a team brings together people with
diverse skills and perspectives. This diversity leads to more

learning, and leads to better outcomes."
```
