# SECTION 1 — GRAMMAR & SENTENCE CORRECTION

This section tests your knowledge of English grammar fundamentals. Questions appear as MCQs — you pick the correct sentence, fill in the blank, or identify the error.

---

## 1.1 — Subject-Verb Agreement

**Rule:** The verb must agree with the subject in number (singular/plural).

| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| The **team is** working on the project. | The team are working on the project. |
| The **results were** published yesterday. | The results was published yesterday. |
| Each of the **developers has** a task assigned. | Each of the developers have a task assigned. |
| Neither the manager **nor the employees were** informed. | Neither the manager nor the employees was informed. |
| The **list of items is** ready. | The list of items are ready. |

**Tricky Cases:**
- "Each", "Every", "Neither", "Either" → always **singular verb**
- "Neither...nor", "Either...or" → verb agrees with the **nearest subject**
- Collective nouns (team, staff, committee) → usually **singular** in formal English

| Tense | Structure | Example |

## 1.3 — Articles (A / An / The)

**Rules:**
- **A** → before consonant sounds (a user, a university, a European)
- **An** → before vowel sounds (an hour, an MBA, an umbrella)
- **The** → specific/known noun (the server, the project we discussed)
- **No article** → general/abstract nouns (Software is evolving. Life is short.)

## 1.5 — Active & Passive Voice

**Structure:**
- **Active:** Subject + Verb + Object → *The developer fixed the bug.*
- **Passive:** Object + be + V3 + by Subject → *The bug was fixed by the developer.*

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Simple Present | He **writes** code. | Code **is written** by him. |
| Simple Past | She **completed** the task. | The task **was completed** by her. |
| Present Perfect | They **have deployed** the app. | The app **has been deployed** by them. |
| Future | We **will deliver** the module. | The module **will be delivered** by us. |

## 1.6 — Direct & Indirect Speech

**Rules for Conversion:**

| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| says / say | says / say (no tense change) |
| said | Tense shifts back by one step |
| "I am busy," he said. | He said that **he was** busy. |
| "We will finish," they said. | They said that they **would finish**. |
| "I have completed it," she said. | She said that she **had completed** it. |

**Pronoun & Time Changes:**
- I → he/she, we → they, my → his/her
- today → that day, tomorrow → the next day, yesterday → the previous day
- here → there, this → that, now → then

## 1.7 — Common Error Spotting Patterns

| Error Type | Incorrect | Correct |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| Double negative | I don't know **nothing**. | I don't know **anything**. |
| Wrong comparison | She is **more better** than him. | She is **better** than him. |
| Redundancy | Return **back** to work. | **Return** to work. |
| Wrong word | He gave me an useful **advise**. | He gave me useful **advice**. |
| Pronoun error | **Myself** and my team worked hard. | **My team and I** worked hard. |
| Misplaced modifier | I **only** ate one pizza. | I ate **only** one pizza. |
| Its vs It's | **It's** features are impressive. | **Its** features are impressive. |
| Their/There/They're | **Their** going to the meeting. | **They're** going to the meeting. |
| Affect vs Effect | The change will **effect** performance. | The change will **affect** performance. |

### Practice Questions

**Q17.** Identify the error: "Each of the team members have submitted their reports on time."
- **Answer:** "have" should be "**has**" — "Each" takes a singular verb.

**Q18.** Identify the error: "He is one of the best developer in the team."
- **Answer:** "developer" should be "**developers**" — "one of the best" requires plural.

**Q19.** Identify the error: "I didn't went to the office yesterday."

**Q20.** Identify the error: "The data shows that there is less errors this quarter."
- **Answer:** "less" should be "**fewer**" — "fewer" is used with countable nouns.

# SECTION 2 — READING COMPREHENSION

This section tests your ability to read a passage and answer questions based on it. Questions typically cover:
- Main idea / theme
- Specific details / facts
- Inference / what can be concluded
- Vocabulary in context
- Author's tone or purpose

---

## 2.1 — Strategy & Tips

1. **Skim first** — Read the passage quickly to understand the main idea (30 seconds)
2. **Read questions before re-reading** — Know what to look for
3. **Go back to the passage** — Always find evidence; don't rely on memory
4. **Eliminate wrong options** — Narrow down to 2, then pick the best
5. **Watch for traps** — "All of the above", extreme words (always, never), partially correct answers
6. **Don't assume** — Answer based only on what is stated or clearly implied

# SECTION 3 — EMAIL WRITING

This is the **most important and scoring section**. You must write a professional email in **50–100 words** based on a given scenario. Evaluators check:

| Criteria | Weight |
|----------|--------|
| **Relevance** — Does the email address the scenario? | High |
| **Clarity** — Is the message clear and easy to understand? | High |
| **Grammar & Spelling** — Error-free English | High |
| **Professional Tone** — Formal, polite, appropriate | High |
| **Structure** — Subject, greeting, body, closing | Medium |
| **Word Limit** — Within 50–100 words | Medium |

---

## 3.1 — Email Writing Format (Template)

```
Subject: [Clear, specific subject line]

Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name / Team / Hiring Manager],

[Opening — State the purpose clearly in 1 sentence]

[Body — Provide necessary details in 2–3 sentences]

[Closing — Mention next steps or a polite call to action]

Regards,
[Your Full Name]
[Your Designation / Contact — optional]
```

---

## 3.2 — Golden Rules for Email Writing

1. **Always include a Subject line** — Specific and concise
2. **Use formal salutation** — "Dear Mr./Ms. X" or "Dear Team"
3. **State your purpose in the first sentence** — Don't make the reader guess
4. **Keep it within word limit** — 50–100 words (body only, excluding subject and sign-off)
5. **Use professional language** — No slang, no SMS language, no emojis
6. **One main idea per email** — Don't mix multiple topics
7. **End with a clear call to action** — What should the reader do next?
8. **Proofread** — Check spelling, grammar, and punctuation before submitting
9. **Use paragraphs** — Don't write a wall of text
10. **Sign off properly** — "Regards," / "Best regards," / "Thank you,"

# QUICK REVISION — Last-Minute Cheat Sheet

## Grammar Quick Rules
- ✅ Each/Every/Neither/Either → singular verb
- ✅ Since → point in time | For → duration
- ✅ An → before vowel **sound** (an hour, an MBA)
- ✅ "If I were you" → subjunctive mood (not "was")
- ✅ Fewer → countable | Less → uncountable
- ✅ Affect → verb | Effect → noun
- ✅ Its → possessive | It's → it is
- ✅ Who → subject | Whom → object
- ✅ "Neither A nor B" → verb matches B
- ✅ Past Perfect (had + V3) → earlier of two past events

## Reading Comprehension Quick Tips
- ✅ Read the questions first, then the passage
- ✅ Look for keywords from the question in the passage
- ✅ Eliminate extreme options (always, never, all, none)
- ✅ "Not mentioned" ≠ "False" — don't assume
- ✅ Inference = what can logically be concluded, not guessed

## Email Writing Quick Checklist
- ✅ Subject line included?
- ✅ Formal greeting (Dear...)?
- ✅ Purpose stated in first sentence?
- ✅ Body within 50–100 words?
- ✅ Professional tone throughout?
- ✅ Clear call to action or next step?
- ✅ Proper sign-off (Regards)?
- ✅ No spelling or grammar errors?
- ✅ No slang, abbreviations, or emojis?
- ✅ Read it once before submitting?
