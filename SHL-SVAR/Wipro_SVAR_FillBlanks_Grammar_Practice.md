```
    Answer: "Poverty is A serious issue in many countries."
```

### Quick Reference: Article Rules
```
USE "A":    before consonant sounds (a book, a university, a European)
USE "AN":   before vowel sounds (an apple, an hour, an MBA, an honest)
USE "THE":  specific things, superlatives, unique things, rivers, oceans
NO ARTICLE: general plurals, abstract nouns, sports, languages, meals,
            mountains, countries (most), cities
```

## Set 1: Subject-Verb Agreement (15 Questions)

```
1. Wrong: "The team are working on the project."
   Correct: "The team IS working on the project."
   Rule: Collective nouns (team, company, committee) = singular

2. Wrong: "Each of the students have submitted their work."
   Correct: "Each of the students HAS submitted HIS/HER work."
   Rule: Each/Every/Either/Neither = singular

3. Wrong: "Mathematics are my favorite subject."
   Correct: "Mathematics IS my favorite subject."
   Rule: Subjects ending in -ics (physics, economics) = singular

4. Wrong: "The news are very shocking today."
   Correct: "The news IS very shocking today."
   Rule: News = always singular

5. Wrong: "Neither the manager nor the employees was present."
   Correct: "Neither the manager nor the employees WERE present."
   Rule: Neither...nor = verb agrees with nearest subject

6. Wrong: "One of my friends are coming today."
   Correct: "One of my friends IS coming today."
   Rule: One of + plural noun = singular verb

7. Wrong: "The quality of the products have improved."
   Correct: "The quality of the products HAS improved."
   Rule: Subject is 'quality' (singular), not 'products'

8. Wrong: "Everybody have their own opinion."
   Correct: "Everybody HAS HIS/HER own opinion."
   Rule: Everybody/Everyone/Someone = singular

9. Wrong: "The number of applicants are increasing."
   Correct: "The number of applicants IS increasing."
   Rule: "The number of" = singular

10. Wrong: "A lot of work are pending."
    Correct: "A lot of work IS pending."
    Rule: Work = uncountable = singular

11. Wrong: "Ten kilometers are a long distance."
    Correct: "Ten kilometers IS a long distance."
    Rule: Distance/time/money as a single unit = singular

12. Wrong: "The staff has been given their new uniforms."
    Correct: "The staff HAVE been given their new uniforms."
    Rule: When staff refers to individual members = plural

13. Wrong: "Bread and butter are my daily breakfast."
    Correct: "Bread and butter IS my daily breakfast."
    Rule: When two nouns form a single concept = singular

14. Wrong: "There is many reasons for the delay."
    Correct: "There ARE many reasons for the delay."
    Rule: There is/are agrees with the noun that follows

15. Wrong: "The furniture in the office needs to be replace."
    Correct: "The furniture in the office needs to be REPLACED."
    Rule: Furniture = uncountable singular + correct verb form
```

```
24. Wrong: "She cooks dinner when I arrived."

28. Wrong: "She has went to the market."
```

```
92. Wrong: "He is one of those people who believes in honesty."

93. Wrong: "Had I knew the truth, I would have acted differently."

    Rule: Had + past participle (known, not knew)

94. Wrong: "She along with her friends are going to the party."

    Rule: Along with/together with = verb agrees with first subject

95. Wrong: "Many a students have failed this exam."

96. Wrong: "The cattle is grazing in the field."
    Correct: "The cattle ARE grazing in the field."
    Rule: Cattle = always plural

97. Wrong: "He availed the opportunity."
    Correct: "He availed HIMSELF OF the opportunity."
    Rule: Avail oneself of (reflexive)

98. Wrong: "She prefers tea than coffee."
    Correct: "She prefers tea TO coffee."
    Rule: Prefer A TO B (not than)

99. Wrong: "He is one of the best teacher I have ever met."
    Correct: "He is one of the best TEACHERS I have ever met."
    Rule: One of the + superlative + plural noun

100. Wrong: "I would rather to stay home today."
     Correct: "I would rather STAY home today."
     Rule: Would rather + base form (no 'to')
```

## Quick Grammar Cheat Sheet

### Top 20 Rules to Remember
```
1.  He/She/It + verb-s (goes, plays, works)
2.  Each/Every/Everyone = singular verb
3.  Since = point in time, For = duration
4.  Discuss/Enter/Marry = no preposition after
5.  Suggest + that clause (not suggest to)
6.  Good AT, Interested IN, Responsible FOR
7.  Senior/Junior/Prefer = TO (not than)
8.  If clause = no will (If I go, not if I will go)
9.  Know/Have/Believe = no continuous tense
10. Told + object required, Said = no object
11. Hardly = barely, Hard = with effort
12. Despite (no of), In spite OF
13. No sooner...THAN, Hardly...WHEN
14. More + long adjective OR -er (never both)
15. Information/Advice/Furniture = uncountable
16. One of the + plural noun
17. News/Mathematics/Physics = singular
18. Would rather + base form (no to)
19. Unless = if not (already negative)
20. Along with/as well as = verb agrees with first subject
```

### Stative Verbs (Never Use Continuous)
```
KNOW      BELIEVE     UNDERSTAND    REMEMBER
WANT      NEED        LIKE          LOVE
HATE      PREFER      BELONG        OWN
HAVE (possession)     SEEM          APPEAR
MEAN      CONSIST     CONTAIN       DESERVE
```

### Common Confused Words
```
Affect (verb) vs Effect (noun)
Accept (receive) vs Except (exclude)
Advice (noun) vs Advise (verb)
Practice (noun) vs Practise (verb)
Lose (misplace) vs Loose (not tight)
Principle (rule) vs Principal (head/main)
Stationary (not moving) vs Stationery (paper/pens)
Complement (complete) vs Compliment (praise)
```
