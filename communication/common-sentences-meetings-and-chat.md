# Common Sentences for Meetings and Chat Communication

> Ready-to-use sentences for everyday professional communication in an MNC environment.

---

## 1. Meeting — Opening and Greeting

- "Hi everyone, thanks for joining."
- "Good morning/afternoon, shall we get started?"
- "Hey team, let's begin. I'll keep this brief."
- "Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time to connect."
- "Let me quickly share my screen before we start."
- "Can everyone see my screen?"
- "Let me know if you're facing any audio issues."
- "Shall we wait a minute for [Name] to join?"

---

## 2. Stand-Up / Daily Sync

### Sharing Updates
- "Yesterday I worked on [task] and raised a PR for review."
- "I completed the [feature] development. It's now in code review."
- "I spent most of yesterday investigating the [issue]. I found the root cause and I'm working on the fix."
- "I finished the API integration for [module]. It's ready for QA."
- "I picked up [JIRA-ID] and I'm about 60% done."

### Sharing Today's Plan
- "Today I'll continue with [task]. I expect to complete it by EOD."
- "I'll be working on the bug fix for [issue] and writing unit tests."
- "Today I plan to start the [new feature] implementation."
- "I'll pick up the next item from the backlog once my current PR gets merged."

### Reporting Blockers
- "I'm blocked on [task] because I'm waiting for API credentials."
- "I need access to the staging environment to proceed. I've raised a request."
- "I'm waiting for clarification from the client on [requirement]. I've sent a follow-up."
- "No blockers from my side."
- "Everything is on track."

---

## 3. Sprint Planning / Backlog Grooming

- "Could you walk us through the acceptance criteria for this story?"
- "I think this story needs to be broken down further. It's too large for a single sprint."
- "Based on the complexity, I'd estimate this as a [X]-point story."
- "Do we have the design/mockups ready for this feature?"
- "Are there any dependencies on other teams for this task?"
- "I think we should prioritize the bug fixes before picking up new features."
- "Can we add a spike for this? I need to research before I can estimate."
- "This looks straightforward. I can pick this up."

---

## 4. Sprint Retrospective

### What Went Well
- "The team collaboration was really good this sprint."
- "We delivered all the committed stories on time."
- "The code review process helped us catch issues early."

### What Can Be Improved
- "I think we need clearer requirements before we start development."
- "We spent too much time on context-switching. Can we reduce ad-hoc requests?"
- "I feel our estimation was off for a couple of stories. We should factor in testing time."

### Action Items
- "Let's document this as an action item for next sprint."
- "Can we agree on a process for this going forward?"

---

## 5. Client Calls / Stakeholder Meetings

### Giving Updates
- "We're on track with the current sprint deliverables."
- "The feature is about 80% complete. We expect to finish it by [date]."
- "Development is done. It's currently going through QA testing."
- "We've deployed the changes to the staging environment for your review."
- "We completed the task ahead of schedule."

### Handling Questions You Can't Answer Immediately
- "That's a great question. Let me check with the team and get back to you."
- "I'll need to verify that. Can I follow up on this by end of day?"
- "Let me take that as an action item and circle back with you."
- "I want to give you an accurate answer, so let me confirm and respond by [time]."

### When Requirements Are Unclear
- "Just to clarify — when you say [X], do you mean [A] or [B]?"
- "Could you walk us through the expected user flow?"
- "I want to make sure we're aligned. Let me summarize what I've understood."
- "Could you share an example or reference so we can visualize the expected behavior?"

### When Scope Changes or New Requests Come In
- "We can definitely look into that. It might impact the current timeline, so let me assess."
- "Should we prioritize this over the current sprint items, or plan it for the next sprint?"
- "That's a good addition. Let me estimate the effort and share an updated timeline."
- "If we add this, we may need to push [other task] to the next release. Is that okay?"

### Handling Pressure
- "I understand this is urgent. Let me discuss with the team and share a realistic timeline."
- "We want to deliver quality work. Rushing might lead to production issues. Can we agree on [date]?"
- "Let me see what we can fast-track without compromising quality."

### Closing the Call
- "Let me quickly summarize the action items from today's call."
- "I'll share the meeting notes and action items over email."
- "Thanks everyone for your time. We'll keep you posted on the progress."
- "If anything comes up, feel free to reach out on Slack or email."

## 6. Technical Discussion / Design Review

- "I've reviewed the design and I have a couple of suggestions."
- "Have we considered [alternative approach]? It might be more scalable."
- "What's the trade-off if we go with this approach?"
- "I think we should add error handling for this edge case."
- "Can we document this decision in the ADR (Architecture Decision Record)?"
- "I agree with the overall approach. I just have one concern about [X]."
- "Let's keep it simple for now and refactor later if needed."
- "That's a valid point. I hadn't thought of that."
- "Should we create a POC before finalizing the approach?"
- "I think this approach will work for now, but we might face scaling issues later."

---

## 7. Code Review Discussions

### As a Reviewer
- "Looks good overall. I've left a few minor comments."
- "Could you explain the reasoning behind this approach?"
- "I think we can simplify this. What do you think about [suggestion]?"
- "This might cause a performance issue. Have you considered [alternative]?"
- "Good catch on handling that edge case."
- "Can you add a unit test for this scenario?"
- "LGTM. Approved."

### As the Author
- "Thanks for the review. I've addressed all the comments."
- "Good point. I'll update that."
- "I chose this approach because [reason]. But I'm open to changing it if you see a better way."
- "I've pushed the changes. Could you take another look?"
- "Makes sense. I'll refactor that part."

## 8. Slack / Teams / Chat Communication

### Asking for Help
- "Hey [Name], do you have a few minutes? I need some help with [topic]."
- "Quick question — is the [service/API] supposed to return [X] in this scenario?"
- "I'm stuck on [issue]. I've tried [A] and [B]. Any suggestions?"
- "Could you point me to the right documentation for [topic]?"
- "When you get a chance, could you review my PR? No rush."

### Responding to Help Requests
- "Sure, let me take a look."
- "I'll check and get back to you in a bit."
- "I've seen this before. Try [suggestion]."
- "Let me pull the code and reproduce it on my end."
- "I'm in a meeting right now. Can I get back to you in an hour?"

### Sharing Updates on Chat
- "Just a heads-up — I've pushed changes to [branch]. Please pull before you start."
- "The [service] is down in staging. I've reported it to DevOps."
- "FYI — the client approved the design. We can start development."
- "Deployed the fix to QA. [Name], it's ready for retesting."
- "Merged my PR. No conflicts with the main branch."

### Following Up
- "Hey [Name], just following up on my earlier message about [topic]."
- "Any updates on [task/request]?"
- "Gentle reminder — could you review my PR when you get a chance?"
- "Checking in — were you able to look into the [issue] we discussed?"

### Acknowledging Messages
- "Got it, thanks!"
- "Makes sense. I'll take care of it."
- "Noted. I'll update you once it's done."
- "Thanks for the heads-up."
- "Understood. I'll keep that in mind."

### Sharing Blockers or Issues
- "Heads-up — I found a critical bug in [module]. Looking into it now."
- "I'm blocked on [task]. Waiting for [Name] to share the credentials."
- "The build is failing on the CI pipeline. Investigating now."
- "I won't be able to complete [task] today. I'll carry it over to tomorrow."

### Requesting a Meeting or Call
- "Can we hop on a quick call to discuss [topic]? It might be faster than chat."
- "Would you be available for a 15-minute sync today?"
- "Let me know a good time for a quick call."
- "I've sent a calendar invite for our discussion on [topic]."

---

## 9. When Things Go Wrong

### Reporting a Bug or Incident
- "I found an issue in production — [brief description]. Investigating now."
- "There's a regression in [module] after the last deployment. I'm looking into it."
- "I need to escalate this — it's affecting [X number of] users."

### Taking Ownership
- "That was my mistake. I'll fix it right away."
- "I missed that scenario during testing. I'll add a test case and push the fix."
- "My bad. I should have caught that during code review. Let me address it now."

### After Fixing
- "The fix has been deployed. I've verified it's working as expected."
- "The issue is resolved. I'll monitor it for the next few hours to be safe."
- "I've added a regression test to prevent this from happening again."

---

## 10. Ending Meetings and Conversations

- "I think we've covered everything. Any questions before we wrap up?"
- "Let me quickly go through the action items before we close."
- "Thanks everyone. I'll share the notes shortly."
- "Great discussion. I'll create tickets for the action items."
- "Let's reconnect on [day] to review the progress."
- "If anything comes up, feel free to ping me on Slack."
- "Thanks for your time. Talk soon."

## 11. Quick Reference — Everyday Phrases

| Situation | Phrase |
|---|---|
| Agreeing | "That makes sense. Let's go with that." |
| Partially agreeing | "I see your point, but have we considered [X]?" |
| Disagreeing politely | "I have a slightly different perspective on this." |
| Buying time | "Let me think about this and get back to you." |
| Saying no | "I'd love to, but my plate is full right now. Can we revisit next week?" |
| Asking for clarity | "Could you elaborate on that a bit?" |
| Confirming understanding | "Just to make sure — you're saying [X], correct?" |
| Offering help | "Let me know if you need any help with that." |
| Appreciating | "Thanks for the quick turnaround on this." |
| Apologizing | "Sorry for the delay. Here's the update." |
| Redirecting | "I think [Name] would be the best person to answer that." |
| Wrapping up | "Alright, I think we're aligned. I'll take it from here." |

---

## 12. Tone Tips for Chat vs Meetings

| Chat | Meeting |
|---|---|
| Keep it short and to the point | Be slightly more detailed and structured |
| Use bullet points | Speak in complete sentences |
| Emojis are okay for informal chats | Avoid emojis in formal meetings |
| "Got it!" is fine | "Understood, I'll take care of that" is better |
| "Can you check?" is fine | "Could you please review this when you get a chance?" is better |
| Reply in threads | Raise your hand or wait for a pause before speaking |
| Tag specific people | Address people by name |

> *Practice these daily. Communication is a skill — the more you use it, the better you get.*
